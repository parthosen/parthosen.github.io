About me

Hi! I’m Partho Sen .

I am passionate about developing good practices in data science, computational biology and bioinformatics applied to multiomics, biological and pre-(clinical) datasets, to study regulation of biochemical processes in humans and microbes. Over the past 12 years of academic research I gained broad expertise.

My research spans between systems and precision medicine along different topics such as metabolism in immune cells and Type 1 Diabetes (T1D), Celiac disease progression, non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD), coronary artery disease (CAD) and exposomics – with a common goal to understand mechanism(s) that incur dys(regulation) of biochemical processes in health and disease states, and thereby identify molecular markers. I am excited to share my vision for scientific research, particularly in approaches that promote the application of high throughput ‘Big’ data, and development of tools to address modern challenges in the healthcare sector, and in cell/tissue biology.
